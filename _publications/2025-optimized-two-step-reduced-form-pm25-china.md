The reduced-form model is a novel method to roughly estimate pollutants concentration in a fast and simple measure with only emission data. This research is based on a longer time span of 62 years compared with present RFMs and rectifies spatial discontinuity and incorrectly negative effects.
- The reduced-form model (RFM) serves as a simplified method to estimate ambient pollutant emissions. PM2.5 concentrations were estimated for three scenarios from 1960 to 2021 using CMAQ to distinguish the effects of the emissions and meteorological factors, including realistic situation, fixed-emission, and fixed-meteorology simulation.
- The two-step method represents the significance of emission and meteorological factors respectively. The optimal meteorology year was determined under fixed-emission situation. Regression model was established between PM2.5 concentration and four main affecting pollutant emissions (Primary PM2.5, SO2, NH3, NOx) under fixed-meteorology situation, using probability distribution function to represent meteorological variation and estimate specific concentration.
- Optimization targets spatial discontinuity and negative effects in the regression compared with present RFMs. Generated an initial background PM2.5 concentration baseline as an intercept to resolve spatial discontinuity and applied constrained regression to eliminate the negative impact in the regression model.
