The reduced-form model (RFM) serves as a simplified method to estimate ambient pollutant emissions. PM2.5 concentrations were estimated for three scenarios from 1960 to 2021 using CMAQ to distinguish the effects of emissions and meteorological factors. The two-step method represents the significance of emission and meteorological factors respectively. Optimization targets spatial discontinuity and negative effects in the regression compared with present RFMs. This research is based on a longer time span of 62 years and rectifies spatial discontinuity and incorrectly negative effects. (Advisor: Prof. Shu Tao, Peking University.)
